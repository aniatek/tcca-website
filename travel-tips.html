Travel Tips & FAQ

Health, safety, packing and practical guidance for visiting Tavolo.

Ask a Question

Essential Travel Information

Tavolo is a remote, tropical destination requiring proper preparation. This guide covers health, safety, packing, and practical information for a successful visit.

Health & Vaccinations

Papua New Guinea has different health risks than most Western countries. Proper preparation prevents illness and ensures you can fully enjoy your visit.

Recommended Vaccinations:

  • Yellow Fever: Recommended for most travelers; some countries require proof for entry
  • Hepatitis A: Protection against food/water-borne virus
  • Hepatitis B: Consider if extended travel or high-risk activities
  • Tetanus/Diphtheria: Standard for travel anywhere
  • Typhoid: Recommended for remote areas with limited medical infrastructure
  • Japanese Encephalitis: Consider if extended visit; rare but serious

Malaria Prophylaxis: East New Britain has malaria risk. Oral prophylaxis or other prevention methods recommended. Consult travel medicine specialist 4-6 weeks before travel.

Dengue & Other Diseases: Mosquito-borne dengue fever occurs in PNG. Mosquito avoidance is critical - use repellent, wear long clothes during dawn/dusk, and sleep under mosquito nets.

Medical Insurance: Obtain comprehensive travel insurance including medical evacuation coverage. Remote location means evacuation may cost you some money. Which is Insurance is essential.

Packing Checklist

Pack light - space is limited on regional flights and boats. Bring essentials and anything you rely on daily.

  • Lightweight, breathable clothing + modest evening outfit
  • Rain jacket or poncho
  • Broken-in walking shoes + water shoes
  • Sun protection (reef-safe sunscreen, hat, sunglasses)
  • Insect repellent and any malaria prevention prescribed
  • Personal meds + compact first-aid kit
  • Toiletries and hygiene items (limited locally)
  • Phone/camera chargers + small headlamp
  • Reusable water bottle and dry bags
  • Passport, insurance, and vaccination records

Safety & Security

Tavolo is generally safe. Remote location and tight-knit communities reduce crime risks significantly. However, as anywhere, use common sense.

Safety Guidance:

  • Secure Valuables: Use room lockable storage; don't leave expensive items unattended
  • Organized Groups: Always participate in guided activities; don't wander alone
  • Emergency Procedure: Know how to contact guides and emergency services. Satellite communication available.
  • Sun Protection: Equatorial sun is intense; apply sunscreen frequently, wear hat, limit midday exposure
  • Water Safety: Drink only purified water; avoid ice unless confirmed purified water source
  • Food Safety: All prepared food is safe; avoid street food or unknown sources

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What's the weather like?

A: Tropical climate - warm year-round (26-31C / 79-88F). Dry season (May-October) is usually calmer, while rainy season (November-April) brings frequent showers.

Q: Do I need my own snorkel/dive gear?

A: No, we provide snorkel equipment. Bring your own if you prefer a specific fit. Scuba arrangements can be coordinated in advance.

Q: What's the internet situation?

A: No connection, However, there are certain areas where a network signal may be available, and you may be able to connect to the internet when coverage is present

Q: How many people are typically in a group?

A: Groups are typically 4-8 people for guided activities. Private arrangements may be available.

Q: What if I have dietary restrictions?

A: Vegetarian meals are easy to accommodate. Vegan options are possible with advance notice. Share allergies early.

Q: Is this suitable for children?

A: Age 12+ is generally recommended, but younger children may join with parents depending on itinerary.

Q: What's the currency situation?

A: PNG Kina (PGK) is the local currency. Bring cash as ATM availability is limited in remote areas.

Q: How do you handle environmental impact?

A: We use reef-friendly practices, limit group sizes, and prioritize local hiring and conservation funding.
